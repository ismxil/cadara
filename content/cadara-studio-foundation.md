## 0. The one-liner (positioning statement)

> **Cadara is a fintech-first product & brand design studio. We help banks, fintechs and financial platforms turn complex money problems into products people trust — and brands that stand out in a sea of sameness.**

Everything below exists to back this single sentence up.

---

## 1. We are

Cadara is a senior, fintech-first design studio led by **Ismail Ahmad** — a product, UX and brand designer from Nigeria, based in Berlin. Over 10+ years, the work behind Cadara has shaped digital money experiences for 40+ clients and 90+ projects across four continents: from global banks like Commerzbank and Deutsche Bank, to category-defining fintechs like Lemfi, gomoney and Cadana, to national infrastructure like the Etihad Credit Bureau.

Cadara is deliberately small and senior. Rather than a fixed agency roster, the studio runs lean and assembles the right specialists — illustrators, motion designers, front-end developers — around each engagement. You work directly with the designer doing the work, not an account layer.

**Our goal is simple:** create a substantial, measurable result for the client — whether that's a Series B raised, a drop-off rate cut, or a brand that finally feels like itself.

*(Framing note: this mirrors Tubik's "We are a group of people united by the desire to… increase their capital… our goal is to create a substantial result for the client" — adapted to a principal-led, fintech-focused studio.)*

## 2. Why we exist (the problem we solve)

Money is the hardest design problem there is. It is complex, sensitive, regulated, and emotional — and most of the products built around it feel cold, generic and hard to trust. The fintech category in particular suffers from a **"sea of sameness"**: rounded cards, the same gradients, the same blue, interchangeable brands.

Cadara exists because financial products have two jobs that are usually split across two vendors:

1. **The product has to be trusted** — secure, clear, effortless, so people will move real money through it every day.
2. **The brand has to stand out** — distinct enough that it's chosen over a dozen lookalikes, and credible enough that people believe their money is safe.

Most studios do one or the other. Cadara does both, under one roof, with a decade of fintech-specific scar tissue behind it. **We are the studio fintechs call when the product has to be trusted with people's money — and the brand has to be impossible to ignore.**

## 3. Who we serve

Banking, fintech and financial services are the core. Within that, Cadara is fluent across the sub-categories it has actually shipped in:

- **Remittance & cross-border money** — e.g. Lemfi (cards, transfers, diaspora communities)
- **Business & enterprise banking** — e.g. Commerzbank, Deutsche Bank, Brass
- **Financial infrastructure & B2B fintech** — e.g. Cadana (compliant hiring/payroll APIs), Salarify (payroll)
- **Credit, lending & financial data** — e.g. Etihad Credit Bureau, Mezovest (SME lending)
- **Insurance & financial education** — e.g. Biscons, FoMA

### Who they are (client profiles)
Like Tubik, Cadara is comfortable across business scale — but always inside (or adjacent to) finance:

- **Fintech founders & early-stage teams (pre-seed → Series A)** who need an MVP, a first brand, and a credible product fast — without hiring a full design team.
- **Growth-stage fintechs (Series A → C)** who need design systems, new flagship features, and a brand mature enough to scale into new markets.
- **Banks & financial institutions** who need to modernise legacy digital experiences — onboarding, transactions, internal tools — without breaking trust.
- **National & enterprise platforms** (credit bureaus, infrastructure providers) serving multiple user groups under one secure, authoritative identity.

### Adjacent / secondary (the "we've done it, we'll do it well" band)
Because trust, complex flows and onboarding transfer cleanly, Cadara also takes selective work in **hospitality & travel** (Motel One), **transport & logistics** (Spatch, EasyDispatch), **SaaS & digital services**, and **eCommerce**. These stay secondary so the fintech specialism reads loud and clear.

### Where they are
The work to date skews **Africa (≈47%)** and **Europe (≈35%)**, with the **US (≈15%)** and **Middle East (≈3%)**. That gives Cadara a genuine edge: emerging-market fluency *and* European/global craft standards — useful for African fintechs going global, and for global players entering emerging markets.

Four offers, each phrased as an outcome, not a deliverable list.

**The Cadara difference:** most clients can buy product *or* brand. Cadara delivers both in one coherent system — the reason a Cadara fintech looks and *works* like one company, not two vendors stapled together.

## 6. How we work (principles)

A short set of beliefs that should run through the brand voice and the site:

1. **Trust is the feature.** In finance, the design *is* the trust signal. Security, clarity and calm come before decoration.
2. **Brand and product are one system.** We refuse to ship a beautiful brand on a broken product, or a slick product with a forgettable brand.
3. **Senior hands, direct line.** You work with the person doing the work. Small, sharp, no account theatre.
4. **Built to hand off.** Design systems and documentation your designers and developers can actually run with.
5. **Outcomes over artifacts.** Success is a raise closed, a drop-off cut, a brand that stands out — not a Dribbble shot.

## 7. Voice & personality

Keep the distinctive, slightly irreverent voice already in Ahmad's portfolio ("aesthetic warlord and design partner") — but pitch it as **confident studio, not loud freelancer.** The tension to hold: **serious about money, playful about everything else.** Credible enough for a bank's procurement team; human enough that a founder wants to grab coffee.

- Lowercase, punchy display type; plain-spoken body copy.
- Confidence without arrogance — let the results carry the swagger.
- A little wit and culture (the "anime watched / books read" energy) signals a real human behind the craft.

## 9. Open questions before we build

1. **Studio vs. personal name** — does "Cadara" fully replace "Ismail Ahmad" publicly, or run as a studio brand *by* Ahmad (e.g. "Cadara — a studio by Ismail Ahmad")?
2. **How hard to commit to fintech?** Full specialist site (fintech everywhere), or fintech-led with a visible "selected other work" band?
3. **Geography in the story** — do we lead with the Africa↔Europe bridge as a differentiator, or keep it understated?
4. **Engagement model** — do we name how people buy (project, sprint, retainer/brand maintenance)? Cadana shows ongoing "design maintenance" is already part of the offer.
5. **Proof we can name** — confirm which client names, logos and metrics we're cleared to show publicly.
